Reflections

Reflection 1

One person who has influenced me positively is my robotics coach, Mr. G. Although he isn’t a teacher, he is still considered a school staff member and volunteers all his lunches as well as countless hours after school to help our team. When I was a freshman, I joined Robotics club but I didn’t find it too exciting, as I never really had anything to do. At the end of freshman year, as I was planning to quit the team, I came to one last event. While I was there, Mr. G. decided to talk to me, even though he did not recognize me, and ask what I would be interested in doing on the team. He personally encouraged me to join the design team. Thanks to him, I stayed on the team and spent the next season as one of the key robot designers. Over the years, I had great experiences in Robotics and I am glad that I stayed on the team instead of quitting after my first year. In all my later experiences with Mr. G, he has been very supportive and has taken time to explain many concepts to me, even with all the other responsibilities he has, in charge of the whole team.



Reflection 3

The most important lesson I ever learned in class would probably be one of the culture lessons from French. For example, when we learned about the education system in France, it was very enlightening to compare and contrast between the United States and France. It is easy to focus too much on ourselves, so we sometimes don’t stop to think about the rest of the world. However, learning about other countries is important because it can help us realize how privileged we are or it could gives us ideas on how to improve our communities. For example, we complain about how stressful school is here, but at least our schools have extracurricular activities, while France doesn’t. My biggest academic challenge was probably in APUSH last year. I didn’t do too well on the quizzes, but I was still trying to get an A in the class. I found questions on tests and quizzes very difficult because there were always multiple answer choices which seemed possible. Also, there always seemed to be too many questions which I had to guess on. Around the end of the year, I studied more for the final than I ever had in my life, and it worked out. I was able to bring my grade up 2% and get an A in the class. It was an important lesson because I learned how to study better, which continued to help me throughout the year and even helps me today.



Reflection 6

I typically wake up around 7, brush my teeth, use the restroom, get dressed, and eat breakfast. I then get dropped off to school by 8 by whichever of my parents is awake. At school, I sit through 5 classes everyday and I leave at 1:05 to come back home. I eat lunch and then usually go straight to bed for a nap. On Mondays, Tuesdays, and Thursdays I have a math class at 3:30 so I have to wake up from my nap fairly early to go there. On other days, I am able to nap straight a bit longer. When I come back from math class around 5, I stay home and do my homework/distract myself and procrastinate, and I usually sleep around 1 or 2 AM. The biggest differences in my routine this year as compared to last year are because of the changes in my school schedule. I get an extra 25 minutes of sleep in the morning as a result of school starting at 8. Since I do not have a 6th or 7th period, I am able to come home and eat lunch at home. Last year, however, I was able to nap for longer because I didn’t have the math class. Otherwise, my typical day has not been very different. I had the same terrible sleeping habits throughout high school. I am pretty happy with my typical day, because I still manage to get everything done, and all the time I “waste” by getting distracted from work helps me manage my stress.

My Favorite Novel

Lord of the Flies

My favorite novel I read in high school was Lord of the Flies. I found it very interesting to learn about primal human nature, especially in a group of little boys. Throughout my childhood, Books, TV shows and movies portrayed a romanticized view of island castaways. I was always really excited about the idea and I thought that I would definitely be clever and resourceful enough to survive on an island on my own. However, this book showed me the difficulties faced by these boys when stranded on the island. Since they had to deal with a social structure, it might have been harder for them than if they were on their own. I also liked the connection of this book to principles of government, and how people must be led.
